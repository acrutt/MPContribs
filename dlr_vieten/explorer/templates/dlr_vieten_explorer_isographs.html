Isographs

  • Experimental data: Retrieved via van't Hoff method from equilibrium thermogravimetric data. ΔH(Δδ) and ΔS(Δδ) determined (temperature dependence not considered),
    with Δδ = δ - δ0; δ0: δ at T = 400 °C, pO2 = 0.18 bar; δ0: determined from entropy fit
    Inaccurate values for δ0 lead to a shift of the absolute δ values for some materials. This does not affect the accuracy of the relative oxygen release/uptake values.
    Experimental data may be highly inaccurate for extrapolated values (indicated by dashed lines).
  • Theoretical data: ΔH(δ, T) calculated from DFT values for the reduction enthalpies from perovskite to brownmillerite, ΔS(δ, T) calculated as a sum of configurational and vibrational entropy and the partial molar entropy of oxygen.
    Vibrational entropy calculated from elastic tensors (from DFT, The Materials Project). If no elastic tensors are availble, the data for SrFeO3-δ is used as an approximation.
  • Click on a Contribution Identifier in the table to open a detail page with graphs for ΔH/ΔS vs δ, and the raw data.
  • To update isotherm, isobar, and isoredox functions, set the variables and plot ranges below, and click on a row in the table.
  • All thermodynamic quantities are given as per mol of O (monatomic oxygen).
  • Please refer to the documentation for more detailed information.
